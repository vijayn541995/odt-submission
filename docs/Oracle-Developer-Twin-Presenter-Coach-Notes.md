# Oracle Developer Twin Presenter Coach Notes

Use these notes with the 5-minute deck so the story feels clear, calm, and impressive.

## Delivery Style
- Speak like you are revealing a solution to a familiar delivery pain, not pitching a science experiment.
- Keep your pace slightly slower than normal on slides 1, 2, and 10.
- Land the headline first, then give one sentence of business meaning.
- Do not read the slide bullets line by line.
- Treat trust and human review as strengths, not limitations.

## Opening Energy
- First 15 seconds matter most.
- Start with confidence and simplicity: "ODT solves the hidden work before coding starts."
- Pause after the first line so the room can absorb the idea.

## Where To Emphasize
- Slide 1: "guided, review-ready delivery flow"
- Slide 2: "before code starts"
- Slide 4: "more than a coding copilot"
- Slide 5: "built, running, demoable today"
- Slide 8: "trust is built in"
- Slide 10: "safer, review-ready delivery"

## How To Sound More Executive
- Use short sentences.
- Avoid too many technical nouns in a row.
- Replace implementation detail with outcome language:
  - "clearer review"
  - "faster readiness"
  - "visible control"
  - "safer adoption"

## Suggested Transitions
- Slide 1 to 2: "Why does that matter? Because the delay is usually not just in coding."
- Slide 2 to 3: "That is exactly where ODT changes the workflow."
- Slide 4 to 5: "And importantly, this is not theoretical."
- Slide 5 to 6: "Under the hood, the model stays simple."
- Slide 8 to 9: "That trust model is also why this matters beyond one team."

## Judge-Friendly Framing
- Say "digital worker for delivery readiness" at least once.
- Say "human approval stays in control" at least once.
- Say "already demoable today" at least once.
- When in doubt, come back to business value over feature detail.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid
- Do not overspend time on OCI or model plumbing unless asked.
- Do not describe ODT as replacing developers.
- Do not say "just a prototype" or "rough hackathon build."
- Do not rush slide 10. That is where the memory of the talk is formed.

## If You Get Interrupted
- If time is cut, jump to slides 5, 8, and 10.
- If asked about differentiation, go straight to slide 4.
- If asked about governance or safety, go straight to slide 8.
- If asked why Oracle should care, go straight to slide 9.
